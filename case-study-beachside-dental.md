## About Beachside Complete Dental Care

Beachside Complete Dental Care is a full-service dental clinic
located in Frankston — Melbourne's south east gateway suburb
sitting at the end of the Frankston train line, 40km from the CBD.

The clinic offers general dentistry, sleep dentistry, and
cosmetic services including teeth whitening — serving patients
across Frankston and surrounding suburbs including Langwarrin,
Seaford, Skye, Carrum Downs, and Langwarrin South.

In a dental market where patients rarely travel more than
5–10km from home for routine care, local search dominance
is not just a competitive advantage — it is the primary
driver of new patient acquisition.

## The Problem

Beachside Complete Dental Care came to Salt & Fuessel with
clear, specific goals:

- Exceed revenue targets by increasing patient volume
- Generate at least 20 qualified leads per month
- Achieve top 3 Google rankings for primary local keywords
- Build brand recognition across the Frankston area

**Starting positions:**
- "Dentist Frankston" — ranking #3
- "Frankston Dentist" — ranking #3
- "Sleep Dentistry Frankston" — ranking #4
- "Teeth Whitening Frankston" — ranking #5
- "Child Dentist Frankston" — ranking #4

Being ranked #3–5 sounds reasonable — but in local health
search, position matters enormously. The top result captures
significantly more clicks than position 3. Position 5 receives
a fraction of the traffic that position 1 does.

For a dental clinic where a single new patient represents
hundreds or thousands of dollars in lifetime value, the
difference between ranking #1 and ranking #3 is measurable
in revenue.

## What Salt & Fuessel Did

### Keyword Analysis and Search Strategy

The campaign began with a thorough audit of how Frankston
and south east Melbourne patients actually search for
dental services.

Dental search behaviour falls into distinct categories:

**Urgency searches** — immediate pain or problem
"Emergency dentist Frankston", "dentist open Saturday Frankston"
High conversion intent. Patient needs someone now.

**Service-specific searches** — researching a specific treatment
"Sleep dentistry Frankston", "teeth whitening Frankston cost",
"Invisalign Frankston"
Considered purchase. Patient is comparing providers.

**General local searches** — looking for a regular dentist
"Dentist Frankston", "family dentist near me Frankston"
Highest volume. Patient choosing a long-term provider.

**Suburb-extension searches** — nearby suburbs
"Dentist Langwarrin", "dentist Seaford", "dentist Skye"
Lower competition. Patients in surrounding suburbs
with no local dentist.

The strategy targeted all four categories simultaneously —
with different content assets built for each intent type.

---

### Website Structure Optimisation

The website structure was rebuilt to give each service
and location its own dedicated, optimised page.

**Service-specific landing pages:**
Each major service got its own page structured around
the specific searches patients use:
- General dentistry and checkups
- Sleep dentistry (a specialty service with specific
  high-intent searches)
- Teeth whitening (cosmetic, considered purchase)
- Children's dentistry (family-focused, suburb-specific)
- Emergency dental (high urgency, immediate intent)

**Suburb extension pages:**
New pages were created targeting surrounding suburbs
where Beachside could capture patients with no closer
dental option:
- Langwarrin South
- Seaford
- Skye
- Carrum Downs

These pages targeted keywords the clinic was not ranking
for at all — representing entirely new patient acquisition
channels that had previously been invisible.

### Content Development — Authority and Trust

In healthcare more than almost any other category,
content must build trust before it converts.

A patient choosing a dentist is making a decision that
involves physical vulnerability and often significant cost.
Content that positions the clinic as knowledgeable,
transparent, and genuinely helpful is what converts
researchers into patients.

**Content types developed:**

**Cluster articles**
In-depth educational content addressing the questions
patients ask before choosing a service:
- "What is sleep dentistry and is it right for me?"
- "How much does teeth whitening cost in Melbourne?"
- "When should my child have their first dental appointment?"
- "What to expect at your first dental checkup"

Each article answered a real question completely —
not as a thin keyword page, but as a genuinely useful
resource that a patient would bookmark and share.

**SEO-friendly landing pages**
Service pages structured to rank for commercial intent
searches while converting visitors into appointment bookings.

Clear structure on every service page:
- What the service is and what to expect
- Who it is suitable for
- Cost range (where appropriate)
- Why choose Beachside for this service
- Clear booking call to action
- Patient reviews specific to that service

**Blog content**
Ongoing content addressing seasonal and situational
dental topics — building topical authority that signals
to Google that Beachside is a comprehensive dental resource,
not just a basic clinic listing.

---

### Performance Tracking and Continuous Optimisation

SEO results are not a one-time outcome — they require
ongoing monitoring and refinement as competition shifts
and search behaviour evolves.

The campaign included structured tracking of:
- Keyword position for all target terms weekly
- Organic traffic volume and quality (bounce rate,
  time on site, pages per session)
- Conversion rate from organic traffic to appointment
  bookings and enquiries
- New keyword opportunities emerging from search data
- Competitor ranking movements across target keywords

Optimisations were made in response to data — not on
a fixed schedule. When a page dropped a position,
the cause was identified and addressed. When a new
keyword opportunity appeared, it was captured quickly.

## Why Position 1 Matters More Than It Seems

The move from position 3 to position 1 for "Dentist Frankston"
is not just a ranking improvement — it is a patient
volume multiplier.

Search click-through rates drop sharply with each position:
- Position 1: captures approximately 27–39% of clicks
- Position 2: captures approximately 15–19%
- Position 3: captures approximately 10–13%
- Position 5+: single digits

Moving from position 3 to position 1 for a high-volume
local search term can double or triple the organic
traffic to that page — without any increase in
the number of people searching.

For "Sleep Dentistry Frankston" — a specialty search with
higher patient value and lower local competition — moving
from position 4 to position 1 captured the majority of
a search result that Beachside previously received very
little benefit from.

## What This Means for Melbourne Health Providers

The Beachside Complete Dental Care result contains
lessons applicable to every Melbourne health and
allied health business:

**Lesson 1: Suburb extension pages are an untapped opportunity**

Most Melbourne health practices have one website page
covering their primary suburb. Beachside now ranks for
Langwarrin South, Seaford, and Skye — suburbs they
were not targeting at all before the campaign.

Every Melbourne health practice has surrounding suburbs
with patients who need their services. Individual
pages targeting those suburbs capture patients who
would otherwise go to whoever ranks in that area.

**Lesson 2: Service-specific pages convert better
than generic pages**

A patient researching sleep dentistry is not looking
for a general dentist page. They are looking for a
page that specifically addresses sleep dentistry —
what it is, whether it is right for them, and what
it costs.

One general "our services" page cannot rank well for
or convert patients looking for specific treatments.
Individual service pages do both.

**Lesson 3: Content that answers questions builds
the trust that converts patients**

Dental patients are often anxious. Content that
addresses their concerns directly — what to expect,
what things cost, whether a procedure is right for them
— reduces anxiety and builds the trust that turns
a website visitor into a booked appointment.

This type of content also ranks for the research-phase
searches that precede the decision to book — putting
Beachside in front of patients earlier in their journey
than competitors who only target booking-intent keywords.

**Lesson 4: 330% traffic growth requires ongoing work**

The Beachside result was not achieved through a
one-time setup. It was built through continuous
optimisation, new content, and ongoing monitoring.

Melbourne health providers who invest in SEO and
stop after the initial setup rarely achieve or
maintain results like this. Those who treat it as
an ongoing investment compound the returns over time.
