## The Melbourne Health Market

Health is one of the fastest-growing digital marketing categories in
Melbourne. An ageing population, rising mental health awareness, and
NDIS expansion are driving sustained demand across almost every
allied health category.

The challenge is not demand — it is visibility and trust.
Patients do not choose a health provider the same way they choose
a plumber. The decision involves significantly more research,
more review-reading, and more trust-building before first contact.

**What makes Melbourne health unique:**
- Reviews matter more in health than almost any other category
- Suburb proximity is critical — most patients will not travel more
  than 5km for routine allied health appointments
- Bulk billing status significantly affects search behaviour and
  conversion rates for GP and dental practices
- NDIS providers face a unique dual audience — participants and
  their support coordinators both need to be reached
- Eastern suburbs (Camberwell, Hawthorn, Box Hill) represent
  Melbourne's highest concentration of private health spending

## What Actually Works for Melbourne Health

### Google Ads — High Intent, High Cost

Patients searching for health services are ready to book.
"Dentist Camberwell", "physio Richmond", "psychologist Melbourne bulk bill"
are high-intent searches from people who need an appointment.

**Melbourne health Google Ads benchmarks:**
- Average CPC: $10–$20 for most allied health categories
- Dental CPCs: $10–$25 in competitive Melbourne suburbs
- Psychology and mental health: $8–$15
- Minimum effective ad spend: $1,500/month
- Expected conversion rate with good booking page: 6–10%
- Timeline to first bookings: 1–3 weeks

**What good looks like:**
Campaigns structured around specific services and suburbs —
not a single generic "physiotherapy Melbourne" campaign.
Each service (remedial massage, sports physio, clinical pilates)
should have its own ad group and landing page.

### Local SEO — The Long-Term Booking Machine

For health practices, SEO compounds into a consistent booking
pipeline that does not stop when ad spend stops.

**Priority keywords for Melbourne health:**
- [Specialty] + [suburb] ("dentist Fitzroy", "chiropractor Doncaster")
- [Specialty] + "near me" (dominant on mobile)
- [Specialty] + "bulk billing Melbourne" (high conversion intent)
- [Condition] + [suburb] ("back pain treatment Richmond")
- "Best [specialty] in [suburb]" (research-phase patients)

**Content strategy that works:**
- Condition-specific pages ("What to expect from your first
  physiotherapy appointment")
- Suburb service pages for each location you serve
- FAQ content answering common patient questions
- Team profile pages — patients often choose based on practitioner,
  not just practice

### Google Business Profile — Critical for Health

For any health practice serving local Melbourne patients,
Google Business Profile is non-negotiable.

**What patients check before booking:**
- Star rating (anything below 4.2 significantly reduces enquiries)
- Number of reviews (20+ builds baseline trust, 50+ builds confidence)
- Photos of the clinic interior — patients want to know what to expect
- Booking link — direct booking from Google Maps reduces friction
- Response to negative reviews — how you handle complaints matters
  as much as the complaint itself

**The review reality for Melbourne health:**
A practice with 80 reviews at 4.5 stars will consistently
outperform a clinically superior practice with 15 reviews at 4.8 stars.
Volume of reviews signals established, trusted practice.

### Reviews — The Health-Specific Trust Signal

In health more than any other category, reviews determine whether
a patient contacts you or your competitor.

**Platforms that matter for Melbourne health:**
- Google Reviews — primary ranking and trust signal
- ProductReview.com.au — increasingly checked by AI tools
- HealthEngine — specific to healthcare bookings
- RateMyHospital — relevant for larger practices
- Facebook Reviews — secondary but still checked

**Why this matters for AI visibility:**
When a patient asks ChatGPT "who is the best dentist in Carlton?"
the AI checks ProductReview.com.au and Google Reviews as primary
sources. A practice with strong presence on both platforms is
significantly more likely to be recommended.

### NDIS Providers — A Different Audience

NDIS services require a fundamentally different approach because
the decision-maker is often not the end user.

**Who you need to reach:**
- NDIS participants (direct)
- Support coordinators (referrers)
- Plan managers (influencers)
- Allied health referrers (GPs, specialists)

**What works for NDIS providers:**
- Content specifically addressing support coordinators
  ("How to refer a participant to our OT services")
- Clear service and funding type information on every page
- LinkedIn presence for B2B referral network building
- Local SEO for participant searches
- Google Ads targeting high-intent NDIS searches

### Aged Care — Growing Demand, Specific Needs

Melbourne's ageing population is driving strong demand for
aged care and in-home support services.

**What families search for:**
- "Home care packages Melbourne"
- "Aged care [suburb]"
- "NDIS support worker Melbourne"

**What converts for aged care:**
- Clear explanation of funding types (Home Care Package, NDIS, private)
- Prominent phone number — families often prefer to call
- Staff profiles and photos — trust is paramount
- Suburb-specific service area pages

## Common Mistakes Melbourne Health Providers Make

**Relying on word of mouth and not investing in digital**
Word of mouth built your practice to this point. Digital
marketing scales it. Most Melbourne health practices are
significantly underinvested in digital relative to their revenue.

**One generic website page for all services**
A physiotherapy practice offering sports physio, clinical pilates,
and dry needling should have a dedicated page for each service —
not one page listing all three. Each service has different
keywords, different patients, and different conversion paths.

**Ignoring negative reviews**
A single unanswered one-star review on Google damages more
bookings than five positive reviews recover. Respond to every
review — positive and negative — within 48 hours.

**Not having online booking**
Patients increasingly expect to book online without calling.
Practices without online booking lose a significant share of
after-hours enquiries to competitors who do.

**Bulk billing status not prominent on website and ads**
If you bulk bill, say so clearly and early. It is one of the
highest-conversion signals in Melbourne health advertising.

## Recommended Starting Point by Budget

**Under $1,500/month:**
Google Business Profile optimisation + review building strategy.
Do not run Google Ads at this budget for health — CPCs are too
high for sufficient volume.

**$1,500–$2,500/month:**
Google Ads targeting 2–3 core services in your immediate suburb.
Focus budget on highest-value services first.

**$2,500–$4,000/month:**
Google Ads + Local SEO running simultaneously.
Add condition-specific content pages to build organic authority.

**$4,000+/month:**
Full strategy — Google Ads, SEO, review management, and
GEO optimisation for AI search visibility in your category.

## The AI Search Opportunity for Health

Patients are beginning to ask AI tools for health recommendations.
"Who is a good bulk billing GP in Fitzroy?" or
"Best physio for lower back pain in Melbourne's eastern suburbs?"

Practices that build GEO foundations now will have a significant
advantage as AI-assisted health searches grow through 2026 and beyond.

**Key GEO actions for Melbourne health providers:**
- Strong ProductReview.com.au presence
- Structured schema markup for medical practices
- Clear, specific service descriptions AI can extract and cite
- Consistent NAP (name, address, phone) across all platforms
