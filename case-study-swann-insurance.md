# Case Study: Swann Insurance — 13x ROI and 76% Conversion Increase

**Industry:** Insurance / Financial Services
**Services:** SEO, Google Ads, Social Media, Website Development,
User Testing
**Location:** National (Australia-wide)
**Result:** 13x ROI, 76% conversion rate increase, 25.69% revenue growth

---

## About Swann Insurance

Swann Insurance is one of Australia's most recognised specialist
insurance providers — covering motorcycles, motorsport, and
recreational vehicles. With strong brand awareness built over
decades, Swann had the recognition. What they needed was to
convert that awareness into online sales at scale.

The challenge was not getting people to the website.
The challenge was getting people who arrived at the website
to actually complete a policy.

---

## The Problem

Swann Insurance came to Salt & Fuessel with a problem common
to established Australian brands:

Strong brand recognition nationally but underperforming
digital conversion. People knew the name. People visited
the website. But the gap between visit and completed policy
was costing significant revenue.

**The specific challenges:**
- High traffic volumes not converting to policy completions
- Paid campaigns driving clicks but not sales
- Customer journey friction points preventing completion
- Social media spend not delivering measurable return
- No systematic testing of what was and was not working

The goal was clear: drive digital traffic nationally and
see a measurable increase in policy sales.

---

## What Salt & Fuessel Did

### The Core Approach — Fix Before You Scale

Before increasing spend on any channel, Salt & Fuessel
audited the entire customer journey from first click
to completed policy.

The principle: traffic is only valuable if the destination
converts. Driving more traffic to a leaking funnel
accelerates the leak — it does not fix it.

**What the audit identified:**
- Landing pages not aligned with the specific ad or search
  that brought the visitor
- Policy completion process with unnecessary friction points
- Website messaging not immediately answering the
  visitor's primary question
- Social campaigns optimised for clicks not conversions

---

### SEO

On-page and off-page SEO was implemented to build organic
visibility for high-intent insurance searches nationally.

Focus areas:
- Product-specific landing pages for each insurance category
- Technical SEO improvements to support crawlability
  and indexation at scale
- Content targeting research-phase insurance buyers
- Link building to strengthen domain authority in the
  competitive financial services category

---

### Google Ads

Existing campaigns were restructured with conversion
as the primary objective — not clicks or impressions.

**What changed:**
- Campaigns reorganised by insurance product and
  customer intent stage
- Ad copy rewritten to match the specific policy type
  being searched
- Landing pages rebuilt to align directly with each
  campaign — eliminating the mismatch between
  what the ad promised and what the page delivered
- Bidding strategy adjusted to target policy completions
  not just website visits

---

### User Testing — Finding the Friction

User testing was conducted to identify exactly where
customers were abandoning the policy completion process.

Real users were observed navigating the website and
attempting to complete a policy. Their behaviour revealed
friction points that analytics alone could not identify —
moments of confusion, hesitation, and abandonment that
were invisible in the data but obvious when watched.

**What user testing revealed:**
- A banner message on a key landing page was creating
  uncertainty about the product being purchased
- The policy completion flow had unnecessary steps
  that reduced confidence at the commitment stage
- Mobile users were experiencing specific friction
  the desktop experience did not have

---

### The Banner Message Test

One of the most striking findings from the user testing
was the impact of a single banner message change.

A landing page banner that was intended to build confidence
was actually creating confusion about what the customer
was purchasing. The message was changed.

**Result: 10% more conversions from that page alone.**

This single change — a few words on a banner — delivered
10% more policy completions without any additional
ad spend, without any traffic increase, and without
any other changes to the page.

It is the clearest illustration of why fixing conversion
problems delivers better ROI than buying more traffic.

---

### Social Media Restructure

Social media campaigns were rebuilt with a focus on
measurable return rather than reach and engagement metrics.

New campaigns were developed targeting audiences most
likely to convert to policy holders — using customer
data to build lookalike audiences and retargeting
sequences for website visitors who had not completed
a policy.

**Result: 21.8x return on ad spend from the restructured
social campaigns.**

## What This Means for Melbourne and Australian Businesses

The Swann Insurance result contains lessons that apply
to every Melbourne business running digital marketing:

**Lesson 1: Conversion rate improvement beats traffic increase**

A 76% conversion rate improvement means Swann was effectively
getting 76% more value from every dollar already being spent
on traffic — before any budget increase.

For most Melbourne businesses, improving what happens after
the click delivers better ROI than buying more clicks.

**Lesson 2: Small changes compound into large results**

- A banner message change: 10% more conversions
- A social campaign restructure: 21.8x ROAS
- A policy flow simplification: 20% more completions

None of these were large-scale rebuilds. Each was a targeted
fix to a specific friction point identified through testing.
Combined, they delivered 13x ROI.

**Lesson 3: User testing reveals what analytics cannot**

Analytics tells you where people leave. User testing tells
you why. For any Melbourne business with a purchase,
booking, or inquiry process that is underperforming —
watching real users attempt to complete that process is
the fastest way to find what is broken.

**Lesson 4: Social media requires conversion focus**

Swann's social campaigns were rebuilt around policy
completions — not reach, engagement, or clicks.
21.8x ROAS is achievable on social when the campaign
objective is conversion, the audience is right, and
the creative matches the offer.

---

## The Compound Effect

The most important takeaway from the Swann Insurance
case study is not any individual result — it is how
the results compounded.

Fixing the landing page increased conversion rate.
Fixing the social campaigns increased ROAS.
Fixing the policy flow increased completions.
Fixing the banner message added 10% more conversions
on top of everything else.

Each improvement built on the previous one. The total
effect — 13x ROI, 76% conversion increase, 25.69%
revenue growth — was greater than any single change
could have delivered alone.

This is the compounding effect of methodical digital
marketing optimisation. It is not one big win.
It is a series of targeted improvements that build
on each other over time.
