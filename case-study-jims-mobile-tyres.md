# Case Study: Jim's Mobile Tyres — 39x ROI

**Industry:** Automotive / Trades / Franchise
**Services:** SEO, Website Development
**Location:** Melbourne and national franchise network
**Result:** 39x ROI, 124% non-branded traffic growth

---

## About Jim's Mobile Tyres

Jim's Mobile Tyres is part of the Jim's Group franchise network —
one of Australia's most recognised home services brands.
The mobile tyre fitting service operates across multiple locations,
sending trained technicians directly to customers at home or work
to fit, balance, and replace tyres without the customer needing
to visit a workshop.

When they came to Salt & Fuessel, they were a startup within
the Jim's Group ecosystem — with brand recognition from the
parent franchise but without the digital presence needed to
generate consistent leads across multiple locations.

---

## The Problem

Jim's Mobile Tyres faced a challenge common to franchise businesses
entering competitive local markets:

- Strong national brand but weak local digital presence
- No location-specific pages for individual franchise areas
- Competing against established tyre retailers with years of
  SEO authority built up
- Need to generate leads across multiple Melbourne suburbs
  and interstate locations simultaneously
- Startup budget constraints requiring maximum efficiency
  from every dollar invested

The tyre category is competitive. Established players like
Bob Jane T-Marts, Beaurepaires, and mycar have significant
digital authority built over years. A startup franchise
needed a smarter approach — not a bigger budget.

---

## What Salt & Fuessel Did

### Website Development

A new website was built from the ground up with two priorities:

**1. Conversion first**
Every page designed with a single goal — converting a visitor
into a booking or phone call. Clear calls to action, prominent
phone numbers, and a simple booking process throughout.

**2. Franchise location architecture**
Rather than a single generic "Melbourne" page, the website
was structured with individual location pages for each
franchise area. Each page targeted the specific suburbs
served by that franchisee with locally relevant content.

This architecture solved a fundamental problem for franchise
SEO: Google wants to serve local results to local searchers.
A page about tyre fitting in Werribee ranks for Werribee
searches. A generic Melbourne page ranks for nothing
specific and everything poorly.

---

### SEO Strategy

With the website structure in place, a comprehensive SEO
campaign was launched targeting three content types:

**Location pages**
Individual pages for each franchise service area targeting
suburb-specific keywords:
- "Mobile tyre fitting [suburb]"
- "Tyre replacement [suburb]"
- "Mobile tyre service near [suburb]"

Each page was written with genuine local content — not
duplicated text with the suburb name swapped. Google
identifies and penalises thin duplicated local pages.
These pages were built to rank and built to convert.

**Service pages**
Dedicated pages for each service type:
- Mobile tyre fitting
- Tyre balancing
- Puncture repair
- Tyre rotation
- Wheel alignment (where offered)

**Blog content**
Educational content targeting research-phase searches:
- "How do I know when my tyres need replacing?"
- "What is the legal minimum tyre tread depth in Victoria?"
- "Mobile tyre fitting vs tyre shop — which is better?"

This content built topical authority — signalling to Google
that Jim's Mobile Tyres was a genuine expert resource in
the tyre category, not just a service listing.

**Link building**
Outreach to automotive publications, local business
directories, and franchise network resources built
the domain authority needed to compete against
established tyre retailers.

## Why Non-Branded Traffic Matters

The 124% growth in non-branded traffic is the most significant
metric in this case study — more than the ROI figure itself.

**Branded traffic** means people who already know Jim's Mobile
Tyres and search for the brand by name. This traffic would
exist regardless of SEO investment.

**Non-branded traffic** means people who had no prior knowledge
of Jim's Mobile Tyres — searching for "mobile tyre fitting
Sunshine" or "tyre replacement near me" — and finding the
business for the first time through organic search.

124% growth in non-branded traffic means the business doubled
its reach to customers who had never heard of it before.
This is genuine new customer acquisition — not just serving
existing brand awareness.

For any Melbourne business investing in SEO, non-branded
organic traffic growth is the metric that best measures
real commercial impact.

## What This Means for Melbourne Trades and Franchise Businesses

The Jim's Mobile Tyres result demonstrates three principles
that apply to any Melbourne trades or franchise business:

**Principle 1: Location page architecture drives local rankings**

A single "Melbourne" page cannot rank for suburb-specific
searches. If you service 15 Melbourne suburbs, you need
15 suburb-specific pages — each with genuine local content.

This is the single most underutilised SEO strategy for
Melbourne trades businesses. Most have one generic service
page. The businesses dominating local search have individual
pages for every suburb they service.

**Principle 2: Non-branded traffic is the real measure of SEO success**

If your organic traffic is growing but mostly people searching
your brand name — SEO is not expanding your reach.
Track non-branded traffic as your primary SEO KPI.

**Principle 3: Website structure and SEO must be planned together**

The Jim's Mobile Tyres website was built for SEO from the start.
Retrofitting SEO onto a poorly structured website is significantly
harder and more expensive than building the right structure first.

If you are planning a new website, involve your SEO strategy
in the planning phase — not after the build is complete.

## Relevance Beyond Trades

The location page architecture principle applies to any
Melbourne business operating across multiple suburbs
or service areas:

- Childcare centres with multiple locations
- Allied health practices with multiple clinics
- Legal and accounting firms with multiple offices
- Cleaning and home services companies
- Food delivery and catering businesses
- Any franchise or multi-location operation

The question to ask: does your website have a dedicated,
genuinely useful page for every suburb or location you serve?

If not, you are leaving local search rankings — and local
customers — to competitors who do.
