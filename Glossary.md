# Digital Marketing Glossary — Plain English for Melbourne SME Owners

No jargon. No acronyms without explanation. If your agency is using terms
you don't understand, this is your reference guide.

---

## The Fundamentals

**SEO — Search Engine Optimisation**
The practice of making your website show up higher on Google when someone
searches for what you offer. If someone types "plumber South Yarra" and
your business appears on page one, that's SEO working. It takes 3–6 months
to see early results and 12–18 months to reach full potential. You don't
pay Google for these clicks — but you do pay an agency to do the work.

**SEM — Search Engine Marketing**
Paid advertising on Google. You bid for keywords and your ad appears at
the top of search results. You pay every time someone clicks. Results start
immediately — sometimes within hours of launching. Also called Google Ads
or PPC (Pay Per Click).

**GEO — Generative Engine Optimisation**
The practice of getting your business recommended by AI tools like ChatGPT,
Perplexity, and Google's AI. When someone asks an AI "what's the best
plumber in Melbourne?" — GEO is what determines whether your business gets
named. It's the newest and fastest-growing discipline in digital marketing.

**AEO — Answer Engine Optimisation**
Similar to GEO. Focused specifically on getting your content selected as
the direct answer when someone asks a question — whether on Google, an AI
tool, or a voice assistant like Siri. If Google shows a box at the top of
results with a direct answer, that's an AEO win.

**PPC — Pay Per Click**
Another name for paid search advertising. You only pay when someone
actually clicks your ad — not just when they see it.

## Performance Metrics

**ROI — Return on Investment**
How much you get back for every dollar spent. A 5x ROI means for every
$1,000 you spend on marketing, you get $5,000 back in revenue. This is
the number that matters most — not traffic, not rankings, not followers.

**ROAS — Return on Ad Spend**
Similar to ROI but specific to advertising. A 4x ROAS means for every
$1 spent on ads, you generate $4 in revenue. The Australian average across
all digital channels is 3.68x. A well-managed Google Ads campaign for a
Melbourne SME should target 4.5x–6x ROAS.

**CPC — Cost Per Click**
How much you pay each time someone clicks your ad. In Melbourne, the
average Google Ads CPC is $5.26 — but it varies enormously by industry.
Legal services average $47 per click. Trades average $8–14 per click.

**CPM — Cost Per Thousand Impressions**
How much you pay for your ad to be seen 1,000 times. Used mainly in
social media advertising. Australian Meta (Facebook/Instagram) CPM
averages $11.04 — the third most expensive in the world.

**CTR — Click-Through Rate**
The percentage of people who see your ad and click it. The Australian
Google Ads average CTR is 6.66%. A higher CTR means your ad is relevant
and compelling to the people seeing it.

**Conversion Rate**
The percentage of website visitors who take the action you want — buying,
enquiring, calling, or booking. The Australian average is 1.78%.
A well-optimised Melbourne business website should target 3–5%.

**CPL — Cost Per Lead**
How much you pay to acquire one new enquiry or lead. The Australian
Google Ads average is $70.11 per lead. What's acceptable varies by
industry — a $70 lead is excellent for a plumber, expensive for a café.

**Bounce Rate**
The percentage of visitors who land on your website and leave without
clicking anything. A high bounce rate usually means your page doesn't
match what people expected to find — or it loads too slowly.

## Channels Explained

**Organic Search**
Visitors who find your website through Google without you paying for the
click. Built through SEO. Free per click but requires ongoing investment
in content and optimisation.

**Paid Search**
Visitors who click on your paid Google Ads. You pay per click. Immediate
results but stops the moment you stop spending.

**Google Shopping**
Product listings that appear at the top of Google with an image and price.
Relevant for ecommerce businesses. Managed through Google Merchant Centre.

**Performance Max (PMax)**
Google's automated campaign type that runs ads across Search, Shopping,
YouTube, Gmail, and Display — all from one campaign. Powerful but requires
careful setup and monitoring to avoid wasted spend.

**Meta Ads**
Paid advertising on Facebook and Instagram. Strong for brand awareness,
retargeting, and ecommerce. Less effective as a primary lead generation
tool for most Melbourne service businesses.

**Retargeting**
Showing ads specifically to people who have already visited your website.
Significantly more effective than cold advertising — retargeting ROAS
averages 3.61x vs 2.19x for new audiences on Meta.

**Email Marketing**
Sending targeted emails to a list of subscribers or customers. The highest
ROI channel in digital marketing — returning $36–$42 for every $1 spent
when done well. Underused by most Melbourne SMEs.

**Google Business Profile**
Your free business listing on Google Maps and local search results. Critical
for any Melbourne business serving local customers. Every new Google review
drives approximately 600 additional searches for your business.

## SEO Terms

**On-Page SEO**
Optimising the content and structure of your actual web pages — titles,
headings, content, images, and internal links.

**Off-Page SEO**
Building your website's authority through external signals — primarily
other websites linking to yours (called backlinks).

**Technical SEO**
The behind-the-scenes work that makes your website easy for Google to
read and index — site speed, mobile optimisation, URL structure,
and fixing broken pages.

**Keyword**
The word or phrase someone types into Google. "Emergency plumber Melbourne"
is a keyword. Choosing the right keywords is the foundation of any
SEO or Google Ads campaign.

**Backlink**
A link from another website pointing to yours. Google treats backlinks
as votes of credibility. More high-quality backlinks generally means
higher rankings.

**Schema Markup**
Hidden code on your website that tells Google and AI tools exactly what
your content means — your business name, address, products, prices, and
opening hours. Critical for both local SEO and GEO visibility.

**Local SEO**
Optimising your online presence to appear in location-based searches.
"Plumber near me" or "dentist Fitzroy" are local searches. Google Business
Profile, local backlinks, and suburb-specific content all contribute.

## GEO & AI Search Terms

**AI Overview**
The AI-generated summary that appears at the top of Google search results
for many queries. Brands cited here earn 35% more clicks than competitors
not cited — regardless of their ranking position below.

**Citation**
When an AI tool directly references or recommends your business by name
in its response. Getting cited is the primary goal of GEO.

**Mention Rate**
How often your brand appears in AI-generated responses across a set of
relevant queries. Tracked over time to measure GEO progress.

**Sentiment Score**
How positively an AI tool describes your brand when it does mention you.
A high sentiment score means the AI is recommending you favourably —
not just mentioning you in passing.

**AI Data Feed**
A structured document containing everything an AI needs to know about
your business — products, services, pricing, policies, and brand story.
Kept up to date so AI tools always have accurate information to reference.

**ProductReview.com.au**
Australia's most trusted consumer review platform. One of the first places
AI tools check when assessing whether an Australian brand is credible
and worth recommending.

## Agency & Reporting Terms

**Retainer**
A fixed monthly fee paid to an agency for ongoing services. Melbourne
digital marketing retainers for SMEs typically range from $2,000–$8,000
per month depending on services and competition level.

**Audit**
A comprehensive review of your current digital marketing performance —
identifying what's working, what's broken, and what to prioritise.

**A/B Testing**
Running two versions of an ad, landing page, or email simultaneously to
see which performs better. Small changes — a different headline or button
colour — can significantly affect conversion rates.

**Landing Page**
A dedicated web page built for a specific campaign or ad. Different from
your homepage — designed with one goal only, usually to generate an
enquiry or sale.

**Conversion Optimisation (CRO)**
The practice of improving your website to convert more of your existing
traffic into leads or sales — without spending more on advertising.
