# Melbourne Marketing Seasonal Calendar 2025/2026

Timing your marketing in Melbourne is not just about summer and Christmas.
Melbourne has a unique seasonal rhythm driven by AFL, major events, school
terms, and Australian financial cycles that directly affect consumer behaviour
across almost every industry.

## EOFY — The Most Important Marketing Moment in Australia

**End of Financial Year: June 30**

EOFY is not just a retail sales event — it is the single biggest spending
trigger in the Australian business calendar. Every industry is affected.

**Why it matters for digital marketing:**
- Businesses rush to spend remaining budget before June 30
- Consumers make considered purchases to claim tax deductions
- Search volumes for high-ticket items spike significantly in May and June
- Google Ads CPCs increase as competition for clicks rises

**Industries that spike hardest:**
- Accounting and financial services (peak inquiry period)
- Office equipment, vehicles, technology
- Trades and home improvement (business asset purchases)
- Professional development and education

**What to do:**
- Launch EOFY campaigns no later than mid-May
- Create specific landing pages referencing tax deductions where relevant
- Increase Google Ads budgets 20–30% through June
- Send targeted email campaigns to existing customers in May

## AFL Season — Melbourne's Unique Consumer Distraction

**Season: Thursday March 6 – Saturday September 27, 2025**
**AFL Grand Final Weekend: Late September**

Melbourne stops for AFL. Consumer attention shifts, foot traffic patterns
change, and hospitality businesses experience their peak trading period.

**What changes during AFL season:**
- Thursday night foot traffic increases significantly across Melbourne
- Grand Final week is comparable to Christmas week for hospitality
- Advertising costs increase as brands compete for Melbourne attention
- Sports-adjacent businesses (fitness, apparel, hospitality) see strong lift

**Industries most affected:**
- Hospitality and food (positive)
- Retail (mixed — in-stadium spending diverts from retail)
- Sports and fitness (positive)
- Entertainment and events (positive)

**What to do:**
- Hospitality businesses should increase social media activity from March
- Plan Grand Final week promotions at least 6 weeks in advance
- Non-hospitality businesses may want to reduce ad spend during Grand Final
  weekend — consumer attention is elsewhere

## Melbourne Cup — Fashion, Dining, and Hospitality Peak

**Melbourne Cup Carnival: October 31 – November 3, 2025**
**Melbourne Cup Day: Tuesday, November 4 (public holiday in Melbourne)**

Melbourne Cup is a public holiday in metropolitan Melbourne only — giving
the city a unique consumer behaviour advantage over other Australian cities.

**What drives spending:**
- Fashion and occasion wear (significant spend, typically weeks before)
- Dining, hospitality, and event venues (peak bookings)
- Travel and accommodation (visitors from interstate)
- Beauty services (hair, nails, makeup)

**What to do:**
- Fashion and retail: Launch campaigns 3–4 weeks before the carnival
- Hospitality: Promote Cup Day packages from October 1
- Use Melbourne Cup as a content hook even if your industry is tangential

## School Terms — Victoria 2025

School terms drive significant shifts in consumer behaviour for family-facing
businesses, trades, and anything affected by parental availability.

**Term 1:** January 27 – April 2
- Autumn Holidays: April 3 – April 19 (2.5 weeks)

**Term 2:** April 20 – June 26
- Winter Holidays: June 27 – July 12 (2.5 weeks)

**Term 3:** July 13 – September 18
- Spring Holidays: September 19 – October 4 (2.5 weeks)

**Term 4:** October 5 – mid-December
- Summer Holidays: Mid-December through late January

**Industries that spike during school holidays:**
- Entertainment (escape rooms, bowling, activities)
- Childcare and holiday programs
- Domestic travel and accommodation
- Dining and hospitality (family groups)

**Industries that spike during school terms:**
- Tutoring and education services
- Childcare and after-school care
- School supplies and uniforms (start of each term)
- Extracurricular activities and sports

## Black Friday & Click Frenzy — November Ecommerce Peak

**Black Friday: November 28, 2025**
**Click Frenzy: Mid-November**

Australian ecommerce has fully adopted Black Friday. It now rivals Boxing Day
as the biggest online retail event of the year.

- Online sales in Australia spike 300–400% during Black Friday weekend
- Melbourne consumers are among the most active Black Friday shoppers
- Google Shopping and Meta Ads costs increase significantly — plan budgets early
- Email marketing performs exceptionally well during this period

**What to do:**
- Begin building email lists and warm audiences from September
- Launch teaser campaigns 2 weeks before Black Friday
- Have landing pages and product feeds updated by early November
- Increase ad budgets 40–50% for Black Friday week

---

## Boxing Day — The Traditional Australian Sales Peak

**Boxing Day: December 26**

Still the biggest single day for Australian retail — both in-store and online. Planning should begin in October for Boxing Day campaigns.

---

## Valentine's Day & Mother's Day

**Valentine's Day: February 14**
**Mother's Day: Second Sunday in May (May 11, 2025)**

Both events drive strong short-term spikes for hospitality, florists, retail,
and beauty services. Campaign timing matters more than budget for these events.

**What to do:**
- Launch campaigns 2–3 weeks before each date
- Email marketing to existing customers outperforms paid ads for these events
- Focus on gifting angle and experience packages

---

## Practical Planning Rule

**For any seasonal event:**
- Start SEO content 3–4 months before the event
- Start paid campaigns 3–4 weeks before the event
- Start email campaigns 2–3 weeks before the event
- Don't start the week before — you will lose to competitors who planned ahead
