# Case Study: Femme Connection — 113.2% AI Search Visibility Growth

**Industry:** Women's Fashion / Retail
**Services:** GEO (Generative Engine Optimisation)
**Location:** Australia (national ecommerce)
**Timeline:** 5 months (October 2025 – February 2026)

---

## About Femme Connection

Femme Connection is an Australian women's fashion brand with over
15 years in the market. They sell dresses, workwear, and occasion
wear at prices most Australian women can afford — with both an
online store and physical retail locations.

Competitors include some of the most recognised names in Australian
fashion: Rockmans, Suzanne Grae, and & Other Stories.

When they came to Salt & Fuessel, the problem was not their product.
It was that the right people could not find them anymore.

---

## The Problem

Most business owners understand how SEO works. You optimise your
website, you rank on Google, and customers find you. That still works.
But it is no longer the whole picture.

A growing number of shoppers are skipping Google entirely. They open
ChatGPT, Perplexity, or Google's AI and ask something like:

*"Where can I buy a flattering maxi dress in Australia for under $150?"*

The AI produces three or four brand names. The shopper picks one and
moves on. No scrolling. No comparing pages. No second chances for
brands that did not make the list.

**Femme Connection's visibility audit (August 2025):**
- Showing up occasionally on Perplexity at position 4 or 5
- Zero results on Gemini, Claude, and ChatGPT
- Competitors already building presence across all four platforms

That is the kind of problem that compounds quietly until it costs
real revenue.

For a brand that started with near-zero presence on three of the

## How It Was Done

### Making the Brand Visible to AI Search

AI tools will not recommend a brand they cannot verify. Before
any recommendation is made, these tools quietly cross-reference
reviews, product data, policies, and third-party mentions.
A brand that cannot be verified gets skipped.

**Three things were getting in the way:**

**1. A 3.2-star rating on ProductReview.com.au**
One of the first places AI tools check for Australian brand
trustworthiness. Generic review responses were replaced with
real, specific responses to real complaints.

**2. No centralised AI data feed**
No single document existed for AI crawlers to pull accurate
product, sizing, fabric, and returns information from.
An AI data feed was built and kept updated weekly.

**3. No Wikidata presence**
AI models use Wikidata as a credibility marker to distinguish
recognised brands from unknown ones. The groundwork for a
Wikidata entry was laid using the brand's 15-year history
and existing press mentions.

---

### Fashion Content That AI Engines Actually Cite

Most brands write content for Google. AI tools largely ignore it.
The difference: AI is not looking for keyword density — it is
looking for content that directly and clearly answers the question
a shopper just asked.

Femme Connection's customers were already asking AI assistants
questions like:
- "What should I wear to a summer wedding in Brisbane on a budget?"
- "Where can I find comfortable workwear that still looks polished?"

Content was built around those exact questions.

**What that looked like in practice:**

- Five long-form style guides published with answers structured
  to appear at the top — making it easy for AI to extract and
  serve directly to shoppers
- Product image descriptions rewritten from generic labels
  ("blue midi dress") to sensory language ("breathable,
  lightweight, soft linen drape") so AI tools understand
  how a garment feels, not just how it looks
- A fabric and fit comparison table published on the site —
  giving AI a clean, structured reference when shoppers are
  comparing options across brands

---

### Keeping AI Signals Current Across Seasons

Outdated information is one of the most common reasons brands
lose AI visibility. Expired sale dates, old pricing, and stale
promotional tags all signal to AI that a brand's data cannot
be trusted — and a brand that cannot be trusted does not get
recommended.

**For Femme Connection:**
- All expired schema from 2025 was audited and removed
- Product feed rebuilt so newest arrivals surfaced first
- Event-specific signals added for Valentine's Day, Boxing Day,
  and Afterpay Day well ahead of competitors
- Explicit delivery cut-off dates fed into site data so AI
  would never recommend the brand when delivery was not possible

That last point matters. Protecting credibility with AI is as
important as building visibility. An AI that recommends a brand
and gets the answer wrong will not recommend it again.

## The Key Lesson

AI tools are already part of how Australians discover brands,
compare products, and decide where to spend money.

The brands showing up in those recommendations are not always
the biggest names. They are the ones that have made it easy
for AI to understand them, trust them, and recommend them.

The window to get ahead in your category is still open.
For fashion retail in Australia, Femme Connection is now
in that window. Competitors who act now will join them.
Competitors who wait will find the window closing.

---

## Relevance for Other Melbourne Businesses

The Femme Connection approach applies beyond fashion retail.
Any Melbourne or Australian business selling to consumers
who use AI tools to research purchases — health, hospitality,
professional services, trades — faces the same fundamental
challenge.

The methodology is the same:
1. Make your brand verifiable by AI
2. Build content that answers real customer questions
3. Keep your data current and accurate
4. Monitor your AI mention rate and sentiment over time
