# Melbourne Digital Marketing Decision Tree

A step-by-step guide to finding the right digital marketing service for 
your Melbourne business. Answer each question honestly — the recommendation 
at the end is based on what actually works, not what's most profitable to sell.

---

one at a time in plain conversation.

## Step 2 — What Is Your Current Situation?

Select the statement that best describes where you are right now:

**1 — Starting fresh**
No real online presence. No website or a very basic one. Not showing up 
on Google at all.

**2 — Have a website but no traffic**
Website exists but gets very few visitors. Not ranking on Google. 
No paid advertising running.

**3 — Getting traffic but nothing is converting**
People visit the website but don't call, enquire, or buy. 
Traffic exists but leads don't.

**4 — Getting leads but ROI feels low**
Leads and sales are coming in but the cost feels too high relative 
to what you're making. Campaigns feel inefficient.

**5 — Running ads but they stopped working**
Google Ads or Meta Ads were working previously but performance has 
declined. Results have dropped without an obvious reason.

**6 — Invisible to AI tools**
Competitors are showing up when customers ask ChatGPT or Google AI 
for recommendations. Your business is not being mentioned.

## Step 3 — What Is Your Primary Goal Right Now?

**A — Get found by more local customers**
Want to appear when people in your suburb or Melbourne area search 
for your service.

**B — Generate more enquiries or leads quickly**
Need the phone to ring or contact form submissions to increase 
within the next 30–60 days.

**C — Increase online sales**
Running an ecommerce store and want to drive more purchases.

**D — Outrank specific competitors**
Know exactly who your competitors are and want to rank above them 
on Google.

**E — Build long-term sustainable growth**
Less focused on quick wins. Want to build an asset that keeps 
delivering over years.

**F — Show up when AI recommends services in your category**
Want to be the business ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI recommend 
when customers ask for your type of service in Melbourne.

## Step 4 — What Is Your Monthly Marketing Budget?

**Tier 1 — Under $1,500/month**
Limited options. Must concentrate on one channel only.

**Tier 2 — $1,500–$3,000/month**
Enough for one strong channel or two modest ones.

**Tier 3 — $3,000–$5,000/month**
Can run SEO and Google Ads simultaneously — the most effective combination.

**Tier 4 — $5,000+/month**
Full multi-channel strategy is viable. Can include GEO, email, and social.

---

## Step 5 — Have You Tried Digital Marketing Before?

**Yes, and it worked** — Note which channel worked. Double down on it.

**Yes, and it didn't work** — The problem is almost always one of three things:
- Wrong channel for your industry
- Insufficient budget for the competition level
- Poor campaign setup or management

**No, this is the first time** — Start with Google Ads for fast feedback, 
SEO for long-term foundation. Don't spread budget across multiple channels 
until you have proof of what works.

## Recommendation Matrix

Find your situation below and follow the recommended path.

---

### Trades & Home Services (Industry A)

**Starting fresh + need leads quickly (Situation 1 or 2, Goal B)**
Start with Google Ads. Melbourne trades searches are high-intent —
people searching "emergency plumber Fitzroy" need someone now.
Google Ads puts you in front of them immediately.
Minimum budget: $1,500/month ($1,000 ad spend + $500 management)
Timeline to first leads: 1–3 weeks

**Have traffic but not converting (Situation 3)**
Stop spending more on traffic. Fix the website first.
A 10% conversion rate is achievable for trades (Coburg City Painters achieved this).
Invest in conversion optimisation and user testing before increasing ad spend.

**Want long-term growth (Goal E)**
Combine SEO with Google Ads. SEO for suburb-specific keywords
("painter Brunswick", "plumber Sunshine") compounds over 12–18 months.
Google Ads covers you while SEO builds.
Recommended budget: $2,500–$3,500/month

**Key insight for Melbourne trades:**
Northern and western suburbs (Sunshine, Craigieburn, Werribee, Melton)
have significantly lower competition than inner suburbs. Earlier entry
into these markets builds authority that becomes very difficult to displace.

### Health & Allied Health (Industry B)

**Need more bookings quickly (Goal B)**
Google Ads targeting suburb-specific searches.
("dentist Camberwell", "physio Richmond")
People searching for health services are ready to book — high-intent traffic.
Minimum budget: $1,500/month
CPCs are high ($10+) — budget concentration matters.

**Want to dominate local area (Goal A or D)**
Local SEO combined with Google Business Profile optimisation.
Reviews are critical in health — 93% of patients check reviews before booking.
Every new Google review drives approximately 600 additional searches.
Focus: get to 50+ Google reviews before investing heavily in paid ads.

**Invisible to AI tools (Situation 6)**
GEO is especially important for health. Patients increasingly ask AI
for recommendations before choosing a provider.
ProductReview.com.au presence is critical — it's one of the first
sources AI tools check for Australian health businesses.

---

### Hospitality & Food (Industry C)

**Need more foot traffic (Goal A)**
Google Business Profile + Local SEO first.
"Café near me" and "restaurant Fitzroy" searches are won through
Google Maps visibility, not paid ads.
Cost-effective starting point: $800–$1,200/month

**Seasonal peaks (AFL, Melbourne Cup, school holidays)**
Meta Ads for event-specific promotions — cost-effective for
short burst campaigns around Melbourne's key hospitality peaks.
Start campaigns 3–4 weeks before each event.

**Want consistent year-round growth**
Email marketing to existing customers is the highest ROI channel
for hospitality. A 1,000-person email list contacted monthly
outperforms $500/month in Meta Ads for most cafés and restaurants.

---

### Retail & Ecommerce (Industry D)

**Drive online sales (Goal C)**
Google Shopping + SEO for product pages.
Ensure product feed is accurate and updated — outdated pricing 
kills conversion rates and damages AI credibility.

**Want AI visibility (Situation 6, Goal F)**
GEO is now a genuine competitive advantage in retail.
Femme Connection grew AI search mention rate by 113.2% in 5 months.
Key actions: structured product data, sensory product descriptions,
ProductReview.com.au presence, Wikidata entry.

**Black Friday and seasonal peaks**
Begin building email lists and retargeting audiences from September.
Increase Google Shopping budgets 40–50% for Black Friday week.
Meta retargeting significantly outperforms cold audiences during sales events.

---

### Professional Services (Industry E)

**Need leads quickly (Goal B)**
Google Ads — but budget carefully. Legal and financial services
have the highest CPCs in Melbourne ($47+ for legal).
Minimum viable budget: $3,000/month for legal or financial services.
Lower competition industries within professional services: $1,500/month.

**Want long-term authority (Goal E)**
SEO + content marketing. Professional services buyers research
extensively before making contact. Long-form content that answers
their questions builds trust and rankings simultaneously.
Timeline: 12–18 months to meaningful results in competitive verticals.

**Invisible to AI (Situation 6)**
GEO is particularly valuable for professional services.
Buyers increasingly ask AI tools "who is the best accountant in Melbourne
for small business?" before ever visiting a website.

---

### Education & Training (Industry F)

**Increase enrolments (Goal B)**
SEO + Google Ads + Social Media combined.
Leo Cussen achieved 300 enrolments (up from 239 maximum) using this
combination — generating 13x ROI with an 84% conversion rate increase
from a redesigned enrolment form alone.

**Key insight:**
For education, the enrolment form and landing page friction matters
as much as the channel driving traffic. Fix the conversion path
before increasing ad spend.

**School term timing:**
Enrolment inquiry peaks happen 4–6 weeks before each term starts.
Launch campaigns in mid-January, mid-April, mid-July, and mid-September.

## Red Flags — When to Fix Before You Spend

Before investing in any paid channel, check these:

**Website loads in under 3 seconds?**
If not, fix it first. Slow websites waste ad spend — visitors leave 
before the page loads.

**Website works properly on mobile?**
46% of Melbourne searches happen on mobile. A broken mobile experience 
means you're paying for clicks that will never convert.

**Google Business Profile complete and verified?**
For any local Melbourne business, this is free and should be done 
before spending on anything else.

**Fewer than 10 Google reviews?**
67% of consumers won't buy from a business with few or poor reviews.
Build your review base before running paid campaigns.

**Last website update more than 2 years ago?**
Outdated websites with stale content underperform regardless of 
how much you spend driving traffic to them.

## The One Rule That Applies to Every Melbourne SME

**Concentrate before you diversify.**

A $3,000/month budget split across 5 channels means no channel has 
enough to work properly. The same $3,000 focused on SEO + Google Ads 
is the combination that consistently delivers the strongest ROI for 
Melbourne SME owners — as evidenced across S&F's client results 
in trades, education, insurance, and retail.

Start with one or two channels. Prove ROI. Then expand.
