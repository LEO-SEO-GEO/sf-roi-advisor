## The Melbourne Trades Market

Trades is one of the most competitive digital marketing categories 
in Melbourne. Every suburb has multiple operators competing for the 
same high-intent searches. The businesses winning are not necessarily 
the best tradespeople — they are the ones who show up first and 
convert visitors into calls.

**What makes Melbourne trades unique:**
- Emergency searches are immediate — "emergency plumber now" means 
  someone needs help in the next hour, not the next week
- Suburb-specific searches dominate — "electrician Footscray" 
  outperforms "electrician Melbourne" for conversion
- Western and northern growth corridors (Tarneit, Craigieburn, Melton, 
  Werribee) have significantly lower competition than inner suburbs
- Google Maps visibility is often more valuable than website rankings 
  for trades — most customers call directly from the Maps listing

## What Actually Works for Melbourne Trades

### Google Ads — The Fastest Path to Leads

For emergency and urgent trades services, Google Ads is the 
most effective channel. People searching "blocked drain Richmond" 
or "emergency electrician Sunshine" are ready to call immediately.

**Melbourne trades Google Ads benchmarks:**
- Average CPC: $8–$14 (painting and home services up to $13.74)
- Minimum effective ad spend: $1,000/month
- Expected conversion rate with good landing page: 8–12%
- Timeline to first leads: 1–3 weeks

**What good looks like:**
Coburg City Painters achieved a 10% conversion rate and #1 Google 
ranking for "Coburg Painters" using a combination of targeted 
local SEO and Google Ads.

**Campaign structure that works:**
- Separate campaigns for each service type
- Suburb-specific ad groups targeting your service area
- Call extensions enabled — most trades leads come via phone
- Location targeting set to your actual service radius

### Local SEO — The Long-Term Asset

While Google Ads delivers immediate leads, SEO builds an asset 
that keeps delivering without ongoing ad spend.

**Priority keywords for Melbourne trades:**
- [Service] + [suburb] (e.g., "plumber Brunswick", "painter Werribee")
- [Service] + "near me" (high mobile intent)
- "Emergency [service] Melbourne" (highest urgency, highest value)
- "[Service] + price/cost" (research-phase buyers)

**Timeline:**
- Months 1–3: Technical fixes, Google Business Profile, initial content
- Months 3–6: First suburb keywords start ranking
- Months 6–12: Multiple suburb pages ranking, consistent lead flow
- Months 12–18: Compounding authority, difficult for competitors to displace

**Suburb page strategy:**
Build individual pages for each suburb you service. A plumber 
servicing 10 Melbourne suburbs should have 10 suburb-specific 
service pages — not one generic "Melbourne plumber" page.

### Google Business Profile — The Free Foundation

Before spending on anything else, every Melbourne trades business 
must have a complete and optimised Google Business Profile.

**What complete looks like:**
- All services listed with descriptions
- 20+ genuine Google reviews (target: 50+)
- Photos of real work — before and after where relevant
- Posts updated at least monthly
- Questions answered promptly

**Why reviews matter:**
- 93% of Melbourne consumers check reviews before hiring a tradie
- Every new Google review drives approximately 600 additional 
  searches for your business
- A business with 80 reviews at 4.7 stars will outperform 
  a competitor with 10 reviews at 4.9 stars

---

### Email Marketing — Underused by Trades

Most tradies ignore email. That is an opportunity.

A simple monthly email to past customers — seasonal reminders, 
maintenance tips, referral requests — consistently generates 
repeat business and referrals at near-zero cost.

**What works for trades email:**
- Seasonal maintenance reminders (pre-winter heating checks, 
  pre-summer air conditioning service)
- "Refer a friend" offers to existing customers
- Before/after project showcases

## Common Mistakes Melbourne Tradies Make

**Running ads to the homepage**
The homepage is for brand introduction. Ads should go to a 
dedicated landing page for each service with one clear call to action.

**Not tracking phone calls**
Most trades leads come via phone. If you are not tracking which 
keywords and ads generate calls, you cannot optimise your campaigns.

**Targeting "Melbourne" instead of specific suburbs**
"Plumber Melbourne" has enormous competition and broad intent. 
"Plumber Sunshine" has lower competition and higher conversion 
because the searcher wants someone nearby.

**Stopping SEO at month 3–4**
SEO for trades takes 6–12 months to build meaningful authority. 
Most businesses stop just before the compounding phase begins.

**Ignoring reviews until something goes wrong**
Build your review base proactively — not reactively after a 
bad review appears.
